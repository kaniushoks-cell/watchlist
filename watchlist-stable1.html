Strategy Guide Last updated: May 2026 +

What This App Does

A mean reversion trading dashboard. Fundamentally strong companies occasionally dip 10%+ below their normal price due to temporary news or market overreaction. You buy the dip. When they recover, you sell. The profit is the gap between dip and recovery.

Mean reversion works best on slow-moving, fundamentally solid companies. It fails when a dip signals genuine structural decline.

Strategy 1 - Stable Mean Reversion

Slow, defensive companies. Utilities, consumer staples. Dips come from macro factors (rate changes, sector rotation). Recovery takes weeks to months.

  • Sectors: Electric, Water, Gas Utilities + Consumer Staples
  • Stop loss: 10-12%
  • Exit: price returns to EWA or 10% profit
  • Review: quarterly

Strategy 2 - Event-Sensitive Mean Reversion

Companies where dips are triggered by specific events: earnings misses, FDA decisions, analyst downgrades. Recovery is faster - days to weeks.

  • Sectors: Medical Devices, Pharma, Lab/Diagnostics, Food Service, Industrials, Insurance
  • Stop loss: 8%
  • Exit: 7-10% profit or EWA return
  • Review: monthly
  • Key rule: do NOT enter within 7 days of a major upcoming event

3-Layer Buy Signal

  • Layer 1 - Technical: price dropped 9%+ below weighted 52-week average
  • Layer 2 - Trend: 1yr AND 2yr trends not both falling
  • Layer 3 - Fundamentals: no failing P/E, D/E, Beta, Margin
  • Layer 4 (Strategy 2): no major event within 7 days

Exit Strategy

  • Watch at 4-7% profit
  • Take Profit at 7-10%
  • Sell Now at 10%+
  • Sell when price returns to EWA
  • Stop Loss at personal threshold (default 10%)
  • Fundamental Exit if company health deteriorates after entry
