Strategy Guide

Last updated: May 2026 +

What This App Does

This is a mean reversion trading dashboard built around one core idea: fundamentally strong companies with stable long-term price trends occasionally dip 10%+ below their normal price due to temporary news, market overreaction, or sector rotation. When they do, you buy. When they recover to their normal price, you sell. The profit is the gap between the dip and the recovery.

Mean reversion works best on slow-moving, fundamentally solid companies — not growth stocks or speculative plays. The strategy fails when a dip is NOT temporary but signals genuine structural decline.

How We Built the Watchlist

Step 1 — Strategy First

We defined two distinct strategies before picking any stocks. Different types of companies behave differently and need slightly different rules.

Step 2 — Sector Selection

We focused on sectors where demand is stable regardless of the economy: Utilities (people always pay electricity bills), Water (essential service), Consumer Staples (food, household products), Healthcare/Medical Devices (people always need treatment), Industrials (essential infrastructure), and Food Distribution.

Step 3 — Four Screening Criteria (every stock must pass all four)

  • Low Beta — under 0.8. Stock doesn't swing wildly with the market
  • Reasonable P/E — under ~25. Not overpriced relative to earnings
  • Manageable Debt — D/E under 1.0 for regular companies; up to 3.0 for utilities (they carry structural infrastructure debt)
  • Profitable — positive margins, consistent earnings over multiple years

Step 4 — Trend Filter

Even if a company passed all four criteria, it was flagged or removed if its 1-year AND 2-year price trends were both falling. A declining trend means the "normal" price itself is drifting lower — which breaks the mean reversion logic entirely. The app's TREND BLOCK signal handles this automatically.

Strategy 1 — Stable Mean Reversion

Goal: Buy dips on slow, defensive companies that barely react to earnings or news. Dips come from macro factors (rate changes, sector rotation, market panic). Recovery is patient — weeks to months.

  • Sectors: Electric Utilities, Water Utilities, Gas Utilities, Consumer Staples
  • Stop loss: 10–12% (wider — these stocks move slowly)
  • Target exit: Price returns to EWA baseline, or 10% profit
  • Review frequency: Quarterly
  • Event sensitivity: Low — mainly Fed rate decisions affect utilities

Competitor groups: Electric Utilities (DUK, SO, NEE, AEE, ETR, AEP, WEC, CMS, PNW) — Water Utilities (AWK, SJW, WTR) — Gas Utilities (ATO, ED, XEL, PEG) — Consumer Staples (KO, PEP, KMB, CHD, MKC)

Strategy 2 — Event-Sensitive Mean Reversion

Goal: Buy dips triggered by specific events — earnings misses, FDA decisions, analyst downgrades — on companies with strong fundamentals where the event is temporary. Recovery is faster — days to weeks.

  • Sectors: Medical Devices, Pharma/Biotech, Lab/Diagnostics, Food Distribution, Industrials, Insurance
  • Stop loss: 8% (tighter — events can escalate)
  • Target exit: 7–10% profit, or price returns to EWA
  • Review frequency: Monthly
  • Event sensitivity: High — watch earnings calendar, FDA dates, analyst changes
  • Key rule: Do NOT enter within 7 days of a major upcoming event — wait for the event to pass first

Competitor groups: Medical Devices (SYK, BSX, BDX, ZBH, EW, MDT*) — Pharma (JNJ, ABBV, BAX) — Lab/Diagnostics (TMO, DGX) — Food Service (SYY, MCD, GPC) — Industrials (ITW, CMI, MCK) — Insurance (TRV)

*MDT flagged: solid fundamentals but weak 1yr/2yr trend. TREND BLOCK active — monitor for recovery before entering.

Watchlist Review Schedule

  • Every quarter — full review of all stocks against all criteria. Remove failures, research replacements.
  • Every month — quick scan of event calendar and news on held positions (Strategy 2 stocks).
  • Immediately — when any stock triggers a red HEALTH alert. The app flags this automatically.

The 3-Layer Buy Signal

Every potential buy goes through three gates. All three must pass for a CONFIRMED BUY:

  • Layer 1 — Technical: Price dropped 9%+ below exponentially weighted 52-week average
  • Layer 2 — Trend: 1yr AND 2yr trends are not both falling
  • Layer 3 — Fundamentals: No failing P/E, D/E, Beta, or Margin metrics

A 4th layer is active for event-sensitive stocks: if a major event is within 7 days, the signal shows ⏳ WAIT instead of BUY.

Exit Strategy

  • ◉ Watch at 4–7% profit — recovery in progress, start monitoring
  • 💰 Take Profit at 7–10% — strong exit window, good risk/reward captured
  • 🔴 Sell Now at 10%+ — mission accomplished, exit immediately
  • ✓ EWA Return — price back to weighted average = mean reversion complete = sell
  • 🛑 Stop Loss — personal threshold (default 10%, set per position) — cut losses to protect capital
  • ⚠ Fundamental Exit — company health deteriorates after entry — exit regardless of price
